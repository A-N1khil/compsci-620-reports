\section{Conclusion}
Generative AI is reshaping software development by transforming how developers explore problems, generate solutions, and interact with programming tasks across the SDLC. Rather than serving solely as productivity tools, LLM-based systems increasingly function as collaborative assistants that support reasoning, implementation, debugging, and documentation through multiple interaction modes. While these systems provide substantial gains in efficiency and accessibility, they also introduce challenges related to reliability, overreliance, and reduced code comprehension. Overall, the findings suggest that the future of AI-assisted development lies not in fully autonomous software generation, but in effective human–AI collaboration where developers remain responsible for critical reasoning, validation, and decision-making.
