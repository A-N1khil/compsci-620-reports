\section{Discussion}
\paragraph{Taxonomy of Developer-AI Interaction Paradigms}
The taxonomy presented in this paper highlights that Generative AI systems influence software development not through a single interaction paradigm, but through multiple complementary modes of assistance. These represent distinct ways in which developers externalize reasoning and collaborate with AI systems during programming tasks.

An important observation across the surveyed literature is that these interaction modes differ primarily in their level of abstraction and developer intent. Suggestion-based interactions operate at a low level of granularity, assisting with syntax completion and implementation acceleration. Conversational assistance functions at a higher cognitive level by supporting reasoning, clarification, and iterative exploration through natural language dialogue. Generative and transformational assistance abstract implementation details further by allowing developers to specify intent while delegating substantial code generation tasks to the model. Retrieval and explanation assistance, in contrast, primarily support comprehension and decision-making rather than direct artifact generation.

\paragraph{Connection of the Taxonomy with SDLC Stages}
The interaction taxonomy aligns closely with different stages of the Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC), showing that developer–AI collaboration varies depending on task goals and abstraction levels. Conversational assistance is most relevant during requirements elicitation and ideation, where developers work with ambiguity and use natural language interaction to explore, clarify, and refine solutions. In contrast, suggestion-based and generative assistance are more strongly connected to implementation tasks, where they accelerate coding through completions, boilerplate generation, and reduced implementation effort.

Debugging and review activities are more dependent on retrieval and explanation assistance, where LLMs help developers understand errors, summarize documentation, and reason about program behavior. However, these systems remain less effective for complex logical and multi-file defects that require deeper semantic understanding. Documentation workflows often combine multiple interaction modes, including conversational, generative, and retrieval-based support, highlighting that AI-assisted programming is fundamentally task-dependent rather than uniformly applied across the SDLC.

\paragraph{Discussion of the Benefits and Limitations of LLMs Across SDLC Stages}
LLMs provide significant benefits across multiple stages of software development by improving productivity, reducing implementation effort, and supporting exploratory learning. They assist developers in generating requirements, accelerating coding through automated suggestions and boilerplate generation, and supporting debugging and documentation through explanations and summaries. Additionally, conversational interaction lowers cognitive barriers for unfamiliar technologies by enabling iterative learning and experimentation. Studies such as \citet{barke2022grounded} demonstrate that developers transition dynamically between acceleration-oriented and exploration-oriented behaviors depending on task familiarity, uncertainty, and cognitive demand.

Despite these advantages, LLM-assisted development introduces important limitations related to reliability, comprehension, and overreliance. Developers may accept generated code without fully understanding or validating it, leading to shallow reasoning and potential correctness issues. Current systems also struggle with complex logical reasoning, large-scale multi-file contexts, and edge-case handling, highlighting the continued importance of human oversight, validation, and architectural decision-making in software development.
