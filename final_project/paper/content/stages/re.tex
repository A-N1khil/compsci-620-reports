\subsection{Requirements Elicitation}
Requirements Engineering (RE) is a critical phase of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) that focuses on identifying, analyzing, and specifying stakeholder needs to ensure the developed system aligns with user and business goals. A key activity within RE is requirements elicitation, where information is gathered—often through interviews and discussions—and translated into structured requirements. Since requirements are typically expressed in natural language, the process is prone to ambiguity, inconsistency, and misinterpretation, making quality attributes such as correctness and understandability essential. Traditional RE heavily depends on human expertise and communication skills, but challenges such as variability in analyst performance and the limitations of static learning methods, like transcript-based exercises, highlight the need for more effective and scalable approaches to improve both elicitation skills and requirement quality.
