\subsection{Requirements Elicitation}
Requirements Engineering (RE) is a critical phase of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) that focuses on identifying, analyzing, and specifying stakeholder needs to ensure the developed system aligns with user and business goals. A key activity within RE is requirements elicitation, where information is gathered—often through interviews and discussions—and translated into structured requirements. Since requirements are typically expressed in natural language, the process is prone to ambiguity, inconsistency, and misinterpretation, making quality attributes such as correctness and understandability essential. Traditional RE heavily depends on human expertise and communication skills, but challenges such as variability in analyst performance and the limitations of static learning methods, like transcript-based exercises, highlight the need for more effective and scalable approaches to improve both elicitation skills and requirement quality.

\subsection{Quality of Requirements Generated by AI}
\begin{figure}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.75\columnwidth]{content/figures/re-ronaki-et-al.png}
    \caption{Two-stage process employed by \citet{gpt_re_ronanki2023gptRE}}
    \label{fig:re:ronaki}
\end{figure}
\citet{gpt_re_ronanki2023gptRE} employed a two-stage process to address the research questions as shown in Figure \ref{fig:re:ronaki}. The first one is using ChatGPT to collect synthetic data, which, in this case, are requirements for developing Trustworthy AI systems. The second method is conducting interview-based surveys. We conducted two rounds of interview-based surveys with different research objectives.

The first step was to formulate 6 questions to create a requirements elicitation questionnaire for developing Trustworthy AI\footnote{The European Union (via the High-Level Expert Group on AI) defines Trustworthy AI based on a set of key principles that ensure AI systems are lawful, ethical, and robust.} systems. \citet{gpt_re_kaur2020review} presents a much more comprehensive review of these principles. Before asking ChatGPT the 6 questions, it was given a context prompt as a conversation kick-off providing some context of the project. The second stage was to conduct interview-based surveys with 5 experts in the field of RE4AI. The same 6 questions that were given as inputs to the ChatGPT were asked of the expert respondents. Once the planned interview-based surveys were finished and the 30 responses from the expert respondents were obtained. Finally, the total of 36 responses (6 from ChatGPT and 30 from the expert respondents) were presented to a different set of 5 RE experts. The expert evaluators were then requested to provide a score for each response across seven chosen quality attributes, i.e., Abstraction, Atomicity, Consistency, Correctness, Unambiguity, Understandability, and Feasibility.
\begin{figure}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{content/figures/re-ronaki-et-al-findings.png}
    \caption{Comparative analysis of requirements quality attributes for ChatGPT-generated and human-generated requirements by \citet{gpt_re_ronanki2023gptRE}}
    \label{fig:re:ronaki-findings}
\end{figure}
The authors find that ChatGPT shows strong potential in supporting requirements elicitation by generating requirements that score highly on key quality attributes such as abstraction, atomicity, consistency, correctness, and understandability when evaluated by RE experts. Comparative analysis (Figure \ref{fig:re:ronaki-findings}) reveals that ChatGPT-generated requirements outperform human-generated ones in most quality dimensions, indicating its effectiveness in structuring and refining requirements. However, the study also identifies notable limitations, particularly in terms of unambiguity and feasibility, where AI-generated requirements tend to be vague or difficult to implement in real-world scenarios.Additionally, ChatGPT and even human experts occasionally fail to capture critical aspects such as safety, accountability, and regulatory requirements, highlighting the importance of domain expertise. Overall, the findings suggest that while ChatGPT can significantly enhance the efficiency and quality of requirements formulation, it should be used as an assistive tool with human oversight to ensure completeness, correctness, and practical applicability.
