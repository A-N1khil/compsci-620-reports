\section{Future Work}
Future research should investigate the long-term cognitive and behavioral impact of AI-assisted development on programmers with varying levels of expertise. While existing work primarily focuses on short-term productivity improvements, there remains limited understanding of how continuous reliance on LLMs affects learning, problem-solving ability, debugging skills, and code comprehension over time. Longitudinal studies conducted in real-world industrial environments would provide deeper insights into how developers adapt their workflows and reasoning strategies when collaborating with AI systems.

Another important direction for future work involves improving the reliability, contextual reasoning, and scalability of LLM-assisted programming systems. Current models still struggle with complex logical reasoning, multi-file dependencies, and large-scale software architectures. Future systems should better integrate contextual awareness, verification mechanisms, and explainability features to support trustworthy collaboration in professional software engineering environments. Additionally, research is needed to explore hybrid interaction models that combine conversational, generative, and retrieval-based assistance more effectively across different stages of the SDLC.
