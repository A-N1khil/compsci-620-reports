\begin{abstract}
    The integration of generative AI systems into software development environments has increasingly transformed programming into a form of human-AI collaboration. Modern development tools powered by large language models can generate code, recommend architectural patterns, explain APIs, and assist with debugging tasks directly within integrated development environments. Although these systems are frequently characterized as advanced forms of autocomplete, growing empirical evidence indicates that their impact extends beyond simple code completion. AI-assisted tools can influence how developers explore potential solutions, structure software systems, and reason about programming problems during development \cite{chen2021codex,peng2023copilot,bird2023copilot}. This work examines the broader role of generative AI assistants in programming workflows and synthesizes existing research to better understand how AI-supported development is reshaping developer practices. The goal is to highlight emerging patterns, challenges, and open research questions related to human-AI collaboration in software engineering.
\end{abstract}
